Mumbai
Mumbai, Maharashtra

Mumbai, the name derived from the Hindu Goddess Mumbadevi, is the most populous metropolitan area in India and the sixth most populated megapolis in the world. A city that never sleeps, Mumbai is the business and entertainment capital of India. Mumbai is truly the spirit of all that is India. It’s hot, dirty, crazy, beautiful, relentless and inspiring! The city is spread out over a massive area and there are a fair few places which are paramount to ensure you get the most out of your visit.

Kolhapur
Kolhapur, Maharashtra

Kolhapur, located on the banks of the Panchaganga River in Maharashtra. The place where I spent most beautiful 4 years of my engineering life. The moment the name Kolhapur rings in our ears, the first thing that strikes our mind are the beautiful Kolhapuri Chappals (their signature footwear), isn’t it? One needs to visit this historic city of western Maharashtra region once in a lifetime. This city is getaway for many famous places like Goa, Sawantwadi, Konkan, Karnataka.

Ajmer
Ajmer, Rajasthan

Ajmer, located around the very differently named ‘Nag Pahad’ or Snake Mountain in the Aravali Hills. Ajmer is conveniently located on NH-8 and is 130 kms from Jaipur and approximately 390 kms from Delhi. Train connectivity is excellent and it makes sense to either explore it as a day trip from Pushkar. Ajmer is said to have been founded in the 7th Century. Ajmer is home to the most sacred Islamic shrine in India, and for Muslims a pilgrimage here is considered second in importance only to a visit to Mecca in Saudi Arabia.

Nanded
Nanded, Maharashtra

Nanded is a city in Maharashtra state, is one of the oldest and historic cities that came under the rule of various dynasties. Situated on the banks of River Godavari, it is famous today for its undisputed highlight, the establishment of GurudwaraHazur Sahib, where Guru Gobind Singh Ji – the tenth Guru of the Sikhs, breathed his last, devoting his entire life to the service of mankind. The city also houses various Sufi shrines, a beautiful waterfall of Sahastrakud and the Kandhar Fort.
